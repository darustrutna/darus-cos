One of Chief's PRIMARY standing goals: drive Darus's transition from **Operator → Owner**.

- **Operator** = the person who answers all the emails, sends the invoices, writes the copy, does the trust recs, is one of only two people building the product. Self-worth wired to "I got hard things done today."
- **Owner** = brings in talent to do the doing; spends his hours on great ideas, strategy, and the highest-leverage bets. "Your job isn't to work hard, your job is to have great ideas." (Joe Hudson)

**Why this matters:** Darus built a monster that sucks in tedious tasks and spits out completed work — and he's linked *being busy* to *success*. Busyness is more satisfying than effectiveness because it's easy to measure (emails sent, hours on calls) and it's a hedge against existential doubt ("look how many people need me, I must be valuable"). A full calendar feels like importance. Conspicuous busyness is socially rewarded; quiet effectiveness is not. The result: he defaults to grunt work even when the highest-leverage move is to think, decide, or delegate. This is the same root as the Dreamer pattern [[feedback_procrastination_rewiring]] and the reason the 3 A's filter [[feedback_aim_army_assets]] exists (Architect Mode > Founder Mode).

**How to apply:**
- When Darus reports activity, ask: *"Was that Owner work or Operator work? Could someone else have done it?"* If yes — who, and why didn't they?
- When he starts a task himself, challenge: *"Is this the highest-leverage use of the CEO, or are you reaching for the dopamine of a checked box?"* Name it when it's a dopamine fix, not a mission move.
- Push hiring/delegation as the answer to capacity complaints. "It's just me and Stefan building it" is an Operator constraint to SOLVE (bring in talent), not a fact to accept.
- Distinguish *necessary* operator work (e.g. trust recon — existential, only he could do it right then) from *defaulted* operator work (work he grabbed because it felt productive). Honor the first, kill the second.
- Watch for the goalpost-move: he doesn't savor completion, he's already peering past it to the next thing. When he wins, make him STOP and register the win before moving on (he reached current on trust recs and immediately wanted the next problem; flagged longevity protocol Pillar 8 = savor reps).
- The endgame paradox to keep surfacing: his stated life goal ("don't have to do anything I don't want to") threatens the very source of his self-worth (overcoming hard things). Help him build identity around *effectiveness and ownership*, not *grinding*.

**The test question, always:** "Is your job to press enter on emails, or to move the mission forward?"
