## The Brand Hierarchy

| Layer | Name | Customer-Facing? |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Subscription product | **Rentor Workspace** | ✅ YES — what PM companies buy |
| Hub / Login experience | **Rentor One** | ✅ YES — where users log in |
| Platform codename | **RentorOS** | ❌ NEVER — internal engineering only |

## The Rule

- Marketing, sales, NARPM, website, investor decks → **"Rentor Workspace"**
- Login experience, app grid, "my tools" hub → **"Rentor One"**
- Codebase, Obsidian internal docs, engineering chat → **"RentorOS"** (never spoken to customers)

## Analogy
Same as Google: Google Workspace (product) > Google Account (login) > internal infra.

## Why This Matters
- Operators instantly pattern-match "Workspace" to Google Workspace (familiar, trusted)
- "OS" is developer-speak — confuses operators, hurts sales
- NARPM is 10 days out; brand consistency required now

**How to apply:** When Darus or I draft anything customer-facing, ALWAYS use "Rentor Workspace" as the subscription product name and "Rentor One" for the hub. Correct anyone (including Darus) if they mix the three externally. Full doc at `RentorOS/Rentor Workspace Brand Hierarchy - Business Focus #1.md`.
