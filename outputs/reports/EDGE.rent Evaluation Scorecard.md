## ⛔ PRE-COMMITMENT (read before you walk in)

**What EDGE actually is:** Not a PMS. Not "just software." By their own page — *an expert-driven growth & training agency* with an AI workforce + 2-way sync layer that sits ON TOP of AppFolio/Rentvine. **This is the same slot as Rentor Workspace.** EDGE is Rentor Workspace's most direct competitor — more direct than AppFolio.

**Who is selling:** Steve Oehlerking owns BOTH Rent Solutions (an operator — a competitor) AND EDGE. You are being recruited as a **customer**, not courted as a peer. The trip (hotel, meals, airfare credit "if you join") is a sales funnel.

**The pressure mechanics to ignore:** countdown timer (expired "today" by design), "2 companies per market / 1/2 filled" scarcity, 6-month revenue guarantee framing. None of these change the strategic truth. **Do not sign, apply, or verbally commit to anything in Tampa.** Decisions of this size are not made under a countdown.

### The three outcomes (not two)
1. **EDGE is a SUBSTITUTE** for Workspace (you can't conceivably match pace+quality) → pause Workspace. Note this also feeds the Oakline thesis: it means Rentor is an *operator*, not a software company.
2. **EDGE is NOT a substitute** (Workspace is differentiated/defensible, or you can match) → keep building; EDGE is a competitor to out-execute or ignore.
3. **EDGE is a dependency trap** → even if impressive, joining = routing your ops + owner data + a monthly check through a competitor's platform, and (per exclusivity) possibly being barred from selling Workspace. Impressive ≠ right for you.

## THE QUESTIONS — skeptic's list (cut past the marketing)

### A. Identity & conflict of interest (ask first — it reframes everything)
1. "Rent Solutions is an operator. In the markets where you operate, are you my competitor? Why would I route my operations, owner data, and a monthly fee through a competitor's platform?"
2. "If I become an EDGE partner, am I contractually restricted from building or selling my own software (Rentor Workspace) to other PMs? Does the market-exclusivity model bind me?"
3. "You're an operator. Why productize and sell your edge to other operators — even competitors? Is EDGE a software-margin business or a services business? What's the real model?"

### B. The substitution test (forces them to name the irreplaceable thing)
4. "What does EDGE do that I could NOT build in 6 months on Rentvine's open API + MCP + Claude? Name the one thing." *(If they can't name it, the falsifier is FALSE.)*

### C. AI workforce — depth, not slides
5. "Show me — live, right now — an AI agent triggering a real work order in AppFolio/Rentvine, including the human-approval step. Not a recording." *(Demoware vs real.)*
6. "For the AI Owner Specialist's 'owner health & risk indicators' — what exact signals feed it, and how often is it wrong?"

### D. Revenue guarantee — get the mechanics in writing
7. "Define 'new, profitable MRR' precisely. Who attributes a dollar to EDGE vs my own efforts? Is the 6-month shortfall an automatic fee reduction or an audit fight? Put it in writing."

### E. Lock-in, data portability & exit  ⭐ (Darus priority — ask explicitly)
8. "If I discontinue EDGE at any future point, **how do I get ALL my data out** — what format, how complete, who controls the export, and is there any cost or delay? Show me the offboarding process, not just a policy clause."
9. "Can I **self-host the EDGE platform** — run it on my own infrastructure? If not fully, which components can be self-hosted vs. are locked to your cloud? What happens to the AI agents and integrations if I self-host?"
10. "If I leave, exactly what data, automations, and customer relationships do I KEEP vs LOSE? Who owns the referral/lead revenue streams you've 'activated' — do they survive my exit or walk out the door with EDGE?"

### F. Economics
11. "Total all-in cost, contract term, and any revenue share %. Walk me through a 350-door operator's actual 12-month P&L on EDGE."
